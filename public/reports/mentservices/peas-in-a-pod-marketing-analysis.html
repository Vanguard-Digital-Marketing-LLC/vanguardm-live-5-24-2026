Executive Summary

Peas in a Pod received 134 tracked calls over the 36-day reporting period, with an 88.1% answer rate and 100 unique callers. Google My Business drove 73.9% of all call volume, making it the dominant lead channel. Google Ads generated 13 call extension calls but only 1 confirmed GCLID-attributed call — despite spending $961.91/month. The account shows zero tracked conversions, indicating a critical conversion tracking gap. 14.2% of calls were tagged as spam, inflating apparent volume. The core local market (Bryan, Clute, Caldwell, College Station) accounts for 82 of 134 calls (61.2%).
Critical Finding: Google Ads is spending ~$962/month with zero conversion tracking. Only 1 of 13 call extension calls had a GCLID recorded. This means we cannot measure ROI on paid search spend. Conversion tracking must be fixed immediately to determine if Google Ads is delivering value.

What’s Likely Happening

Google Ads is driving clicks (615/month at a reasonable $1.56 CPC for childcare), but the conversion pipeline is broken:

1. GTM container GTM-W5Q7T2MW may not have a Google Ads conversion tag firing on calls.
2. Nimbata’s number pool may not be passing GCLIDs correctly to call records.
3. The Google Ads account may not have an active conversion action for phone calls.
4. Call extension numbers may not match Nimbata tracking numbers.

Call Handling Assessment

The 59% destination-hangup rate is healthy — it means the business is typically the one ending calls after handling the inquiry. However, the 41% caller-hangup rate warrants attention: some callers may be hitting voicemail (the IVR system asks callers to press 1 for the front office) and hanging up before leaving a message. The 11.9% missed call rate (16 calls) represents potential lost business, particularly for a childcare center where parents may call competitors if they don’t get an immediate answer.

Prioritized Action Plan

1

Fix Google Ads Conversion Tracking

Audit GTM container GTM-W5Q7T2MW for active conversion tags. Verify Nimbata GCLID passback to Google Ads. Create/verify phone call conversion action in the Ads account. This is blocking all ROI measurement on $962/month in spend.

2

Tighten Google Ads Geo-Targeting

Restrict campaign radius to 25–30 miles around College Station, TX 77845. Exclude out-of-state and distant Texas cities (Clute, Sweeny, Corsicana) that are unlikely to convert for a local daycare. This will reduce wasted click spend immediately.

3

Reduce Spam Call Volume

19 spam calls (14.2%) inflate metrics and waste staff time. Consider adding Nimbata’s spam detection filters, implementing a call screening IVR, or using Google Ads call-only ads with verified numbers to reduce robocall volume.

4

Optimize Google My Business Profile

GMB drives 73.9% of all calls — it’s the #1 lead channel. Invest in weekly GMB posts, updated photos, parent FAQ responses, and review generation to maintain and grow this organic channel.

5

Reduce Missed Calls (11.9%)

16 missed calls represent potential lost enrollments. Review IVR flow (currently requires pressing 1), consider auto-callback or SMS follow-up for missed calls, and ensure adequate Tuesday staffing when volume peaks.

6

Close the Callback Loop

9 calls were tagged “Call Back Requested.” Implement a systematic callback tracking process to ensure every callback request is returned within 2 hours. Lost callbacks are lost enrollments.

7

Improve Call Rating Coverage

Only 20 of 134 calls have quality ratings. Establish a standard for rating every call to enable data-driven staffing and training decisions. Target 80%+ rating coverage within 30 days.
